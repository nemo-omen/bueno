About

I'm Jeff Caldwell, a newcomer to web development who started learning about a year ago (sometime in September of 2019).

Actually, over the past twenty years I've dabbled in various parts of web design and development but didn't ever make a serious and concerted effort at learning until this last year. One thing that I enjoyed about learning all those years ago was the simplicity of making things for the web. If you wanted to show people a page all you had to do was create the page, include the links to your styles and scripts, and you were up and running.

My first site was a portfolio of my drawings and sketches and the first time I saw my own work on a computer screen I was amazed at my own ability to create and share something with the world online. Adding interactivity to the front-end was usually a matter of adding a little JavaScript to achieve things that weren't built-in to HTML.

Now, 20 years later, approaching the front-end is a much more complicated and daunting affair for newcomers. At least, that is, if you follow the latest practices in front-end development.
